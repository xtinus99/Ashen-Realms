HOUSE VANDER (NPCs)
======================================================================

----- Tarrin Vander (tarrin-vander) -----

Role: Younger child of House Vander, scholar · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## Who He Is

The younger of Lord Theron Vander’s two children — a slight, ink-stained young man of nineteen with a scholar’s stoop and a noble’s corrected posture fighting each other for control of his spine. He keeps no formal house duties. He keeps books. He is a fixture at the Docent’s Archive and one of the few people in Crownfall who can read Brancalic on sight — fluently enough to translate it on commission.

 ## The One Who Read the Boxes

Tarrin is the only Vander in three generations who actually went through the family’s old papers, and he had quietly pieced together more of the truth than anyone living: a gap in the family register around the move to Crownfall, three or four ancestors who must have existed but were never named, and a founder’s letter forbidding the family from ever going west. He had carried it alone for years, waiting — he couldn’t have said for what — until three strangers arrived at his father’s door with an iron-flower crest and a dead woman’s name.

 ## West, With the Party

Lord Theron sent him to Twin Reach with the trio rather than his sister — the scholar over the heir. His blood opened the sealed vault door where the family’s old line had ended a hundred and eighty years before. Inside, he read the recovered records book before anyone else could; its contents are in his head now whether or not it is ever read again. He took the household’s dead home with him — and the remains of the never-born child found in the dark — meaning to lay all of them to rest properly, with their names.

 > “There’s a gap in our register, around the move. Three or four people who must have existed. Even families that lose people name them. I’ve wondered about that for years.”

 ## Manner

Quiet, careful with his words, a little socially soft. He doesn’t argue — he asks questions back. Press him and he produces a document rather than an opinion, because that is where he is surest of himself. His elder sister is the more gifted scholar; he knows it, and tries harder anyway.

----- Lord Theron Vander (lord-theron-vander) -----

Role: Lord of House Vander · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## Who He Is

The head of House Vander — a minor Crownfall line, six generations in the city, respectable and not rich, nowhere near the Ten Coinholders. Late forties, a neat greying beard, the iron-flower crest pinned at his breast on principle more than pride. A measured, tired-courteous man who has spent his life keeping the family from sliding any further down the ladder.

 ## The Story He Grew Up With

Theron inherited a clean, tender version of the family’s origin: long ago the Vanders died in a war at a western waystation, a four-year-old boy was saved by his nanny and brought to Crownfall, and from him the whole line descends. With it came an oath, six generations unbroken — we do not go west — and a vague sense that the family had once been larger and richer than the modest house he runs today.

 ## What the Party Brought Him

The trio arrived with proof the story was incomplete: an iron-flower crest recovered from the waystation itself, the name Mara, and an account of his own ancestor named aloud in the ruins. He heard them out, asked for evidence, and — once he could not pretend the crest was not his — authorised the recovery and sent his son Tarrin west in his place. What came back from that vault rewrote the gentle story he was raised on. He was not the same man after it.

 > “My ancestor was a child when his family died. That’s the story I grew up with. If you’re telling me there is more to it — and you have proof — then we should sit down.”

----- Iselle Vander (iselle-vander) -----

Role: Heir apparent of House Vander · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## Who She Is

Lord Theron’s elder child and heir, twenty-two, and by every practical measure the one who actually runs House Vander — she has held the family’s accounts and correspondence since she was eighteen. Sharp, plainspoken, and quick to notice when she is being managed. She learned Brancalic properly, at the Archive, and reads a room continuously. Her father holds the title; she holds the ledgers.

 ## Standing on House Grounds

When the trio’s business carried her father’s household west, it was her younger brother Tarrin who went and Iselle who stayed to keep the house. The party’s dealings ran through Theron and Tarrin; Iselle they knew mostly by reputation — the harder, more practical Vander mind, the one who would have asked who sent them and what the sender wanted before her father had finished being polite.

 > “My family doesn’t have western holdings worth recovering. Not on paper, anyway. So if you’re telling me there’s a sealed Vander vault three days west, I have two questions: who sent you, and what do they want out of it?”

----- Halric Vander (halric-vander) -----

Role: The bound son of the old Vander line · Location: The Twin Reach vault · Status: Laid to rest

 ## The Son Who Bled in the Dark

Mara Vander’s eldest child and the heir-in-waiting of the old, pre-Crownfall Vander line. When a raiding-war reached the family’s western waystation a hundred and eighty years ago, Halric — barely out of his teens — took a deep wound and fled down the sunken stair to the family vault. He bled out there, alone, behind a sealed adamantine door, and his grief and rage did not rest with him. His bones lay curled against the chamber wall for a hundred and eighty years.

 ## The Stand

When Tarrin’s blood opened that door, Halric rose — a spectral knight in a ruined iron-flower breastplate, sword and shield of cold light, the death-wound still open at his side, a century and a half of fury in a face no older than the cousin who had freed him. He froze at the sight of a living Vander, half-knowing the blood. The party never got the chance to reach him with a name; the fight was already on, and they put him down. His bones dropped back to the wall.

He was the “brother” the little apparition in the ruins kept asking after — the half-sibling she never met, whose shape the haunting had given her. He fell the same hour she had asked Farkas after him.

 ## What He Left

A masterwork Vander Heritage Longsword with the iron flower worked into its cross-guard, an Iron-Flower Shield bearing the family arms, and a breastplate too far rotted to wear. Tarrin claimed all of it — to lay Halric’s bones, and the rest of the household dead, properly, with their names.

----- Mara Vander (mara-vander) -----

Role: Daughter of the old Vander line · Location: The Twin Reach waystation · Status: Long dead

 ## The Mother in the Corridor

A young noblewoman of House Vander, not yet thirty, who died at the Twin Reach waystation the night a raiding-war reached it — a hundred and eighty years ago. The party never met her in life. They met her in the ruin’s residue: a figure walking the corridor in total dissociation, barefoot, hair undone, a vacant smile, carrying a small wrapped bundle as though it were laundry.

 ## The Secret She Carried

Mara had been hiding a pregnancy from the household — not her young son, who was openly hers, but a second, secret child whose father was never named. It came to term in the chaos of the family’s flight to the waystation, and in the privacy of the family vault she gave it up to the dark. The wrapped bundle the party found sealed in that vault was that child. A few hours later the raiders came, and Mara died with almost everyone else.

For a hundred and eighty years House Vander did not even remember her name — only a gap in the register where she should have been. The party carried it back to her descendants. To the living Vanders, Mara was new information.

----- Aldous Vander (aldous-vander) -----

Role: Patriarch of the old Vander line · Location: The Twin Reach waystation · Status: Long dead

 ## The Grandfather at the Door

The patriarch of House Vander in the years before Crownfall — Mara’s father, an old man by the standards of his day, “Grandpapa” to the household’s children. The family then held a western estate and a waystation on the old Sprawl-trade road, and Aldous had spent decades of his life compiling the family’s trade-and-lineage register — the very book that was sealed inside the vault.

 ## The Last Night

On the night the household fled to the waystation, Aldous discovered what his daughter meant to do. The party saw the confrontation replayed in the ruins — a translucent, colour-leached old man in the main hall, gesturing in fury at a daughter who would not look at him:

 > “You can’t keep doing this, Mara — the Vander line will not bear it.”

It was the moment that gave the party the family’s name. Aldous died in the raid hours later, defending the household. His afterimage still lingers by the eastern doorpost where he fell.

----- Joran Vander (joran-vander) -----

Role: The boy who was saved; founder of the Crownfall Vander line · Location: Crownfall · Status: Long dead

 ## The Boy Who Was Saved

Joran was four years old the night his family died at the Twin Reach waystation. His nanny, Brenna, smuggled him out a side passage as the raiders came in and walked him to Crownfall over the days that followed, where distant Vander cousins took him in. He grew up, married, and fathered the line that runs down to Lord Theron and his children today. Of everyone in the household that night, Joran alone survived — and every living Vander’s blood opens the vault door because of it.

 ## The One Expedition

At twenty-three he led a single expedition west, six men with him, to find the waystation and bury his family. He found it — the iron-flower crest still legible under the moss — and went down to the sealed door. He did not open it. That night the ruin’s residue played for him: his mother and her bundle, a small girl asking if he was the brother she had been waiting for, his grandfather shouting Mara’s name. He lost two of his men to the household’s shades and took a wound that left his arm trembling for the rest of his life.

 ## The Oath

He came home a changed man and never spoke of the trip in any detail. He left a single letter to his eldest son — the prohibition, and the names of the two men he had lost, and nothing else — and made his children swear never to go west. They swore. The oath held for six generations, through two more failed defiances that ended in a suicide and a haunted retreat, until the party finally gave House Vander a reason the founder never had: that the haunting could be survived, and the dead brought home.

 > “The family died there. The family stays dead. You will not go west. None of you will ever go west. I have looked, and what is there is not for us.”

----- Delvin (delvin) -----

Role: House Vander household guard · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## Bring My Son Back

A career household guard, late forties, about ten years in Lord Theron Vander’s service — plate with the iron flower etched at the breast, a longsword and a cased heavy crossbow, and a flat soldier’s gaze that reads a room once and then watches the exits. When Theron sent his son Tarrin west to the Twin Reach vault with the trio, Delvin went as the boy’s escort. His standing order was four words — “bring my son back” — and as far as Delvin is concerned, that is the whole of his job.

 ## The Twin Reach Run

He met the party at the West Gate at dawn, read them once, and got on with it: kept the flanks on the road, let them set the pace, and planted himself between Tarrin and every sign of trouble — including the distant boom of the falling Hexwright’s Tower (“a long way off, young master. Not our road.”). At the vault he held the corridor with Tarrin rather than make the family’s business his — but when the wraith dropped both Anvil and Ratsby, it was Delvin who kept them breathing, stabilising both with Tarrin’s healing supplies. He brought the young master home. That was the job, and he did it.

----- Naya Selvik (naya-selvik) -----

Role: Head of House Selvik, the Veilwalkers · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## The Veilwalker

The head of House Selvik — the Veilwalkers, the Coinholder house that runs salvage and expeditions into the deep Unclaimed. Naya has been farther out there than almost anyone alive, and it shows: wiry, weather-beaten, two fingers lost to frostbite in some time-locked zone, older in the face than her years. She took her first expedition at sixteen and led the worst of them at forty; the survivors of that one don’t talk about it, and neither does she. She drinks but never seems drunk, speaks rarely, and is listened to when she does.

 ## The Hand Behind the Contracts

The party have never had to sit across from her, but every Selvik job traces back to her desk. It was Naya who put Korven Strake on Ratsby, and Naya whose long game set the Twin Reach contract in motion. She trusts almost no one and interferes with her operators almost never — when House Selvik wants something it commits, and it does not forget a debt or a runner.

----- Steward Mavris (steward-mavris) -----

Role: Steward of Contracts, House Selvik · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## The Face of the Contract

House Selvik’s contracts steward — a warm, talkative man in his mid-fifties in the house’s black-and-grey, behind a ledger-hung desk with a pot of tea always going. He was the face the party dealt with for the Twin Reach job: friendly, unhurried, genuinely good company, and an absolute master of telling you a great deal while disclosing nothing the house doesn’t want disclosed. The withholding is buried under the chat.

 ## What He Wasn’t Saying

Mavris knew from the start what the sealed vault really held — a Conquest-era trade-and-lineage records book House Selvik had been quietly chasing for decades, ever since their surveyors matched an iron-flower boundary stone to House Vander a hundred years ago. He handed over the contract terms cleanly — 300 gold and a thirty-percent share of salvage — and let the party find out the rest for themselves. When they came back having learned it — the Vander name, the family records, Selvik’s commercial interest — he didn’t pretend otherwise. The lie was passive; he doesn’t double down once it’s exposed.

 > “The contract specifies recovery of a sealed item from a pre-Conquest waystation. It specifies the gold and the salvage share. It does not specify how. That is the operator’s matter.”

----- Korven Strake (korven-strake) -----

Role: House Selvik recovery specialist — “The Last Walk” · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active (Ratsby contract closed)

 ## The Last Walk

House Selvik’s premier urban recovery operator — the man they send when a thief or a runner has gone to ground inside the city. Wiry and walking-shaped rather than fighting-shaped, and Unclaimed-scarred: a time-stretched left cheek that pulls his face into a permanent half-grin, a shadow-burn at one temple, and a left eye that blinks a half-beat out of rhythm with the right — once you notice it, you can’t stop. He doesn’t run; he walks, and covers more ground than anyone because he never stops. He hasn’t failed a city recovery in fourteen years.

 ## He Reads the Gait

A target can change clothes, dye hair, mask scent — but Korven hunts the way you walk, and that is far harder to fake. It is how he found Ratsby. Hired on the 12th of Hathren to recover the goblin and the coin he’d lifted from Salvager’s Square, Korven ran him down at the Common Cup and closed the contract, streak intact. Ratsby was held; Anvil bailed him out. There’s no active hunt now and no mark on him — but Korven knows Ratsby’s gait is on file, and Anvil is owed the bail.

 ## Manner

Sparse, cold, never cruel and never hurried. With small prey he turns theatrical — sits down uninvited, gives the asymmetric grin, names your alias and your take, and offers to buy you a drink while you decide. With anything he reckons genuinely dangerous, the grin vanishes — and that is the tell worth watching: if the grin disappears, you have just been reclassified.

----- Mor (mor) -----

Role: Worker at the Silken Refuge · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## Who She Is

A wiry goblin woman in her late twenties, sharp-eyed and faintly amused, a thin scar across the bridge of her nose and a small bone hairpin holding back her cropped dark hair. An Ashford refugee — she fled the burning city and was scooped up by Crownfall’s slave market on her second day; Madame Veshra Coil bought and freed her on the third. She stays at the Silken Refuge by choice.

 ## Manner

Biting humour in a Crownfall street-cadence with a goblin lilt, teasing without cruelty, with a particular soft spot for small-folk who rarely see one of their own in a place like the Refuge. She reads a person like a question she’s already answered. She will not talk about Ashford, and her grin goes quieter when she’s actually enjoying herself. Ratsby found her good company.

----- Veil (veil) -----

Role: Worker at the Silken Refuge · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## Who She Is

An air genasi of apparent late twenties — crystal-pale skin with the barest blue undertone, long white-silver hair that drifts upward and sideways even in still air, eyes the colour of a clear winter sky, and faint pale-blue line-patterns traced along her temples and collarbones like cloud-script. Before the Silken Refuge she was kept as a “weather instrument” in some Sovereign-adjacent court; Madame Veshra got her out of it, and the precise how is left unspoken.

 ## Manner

Quiet, contemplative, half-otherworldly — she speaks in a soft voice the room seems to lean toward. People who come to her wanting to feel rare tend to leave saying they were the rare thing. Farkas spent his first real night in Crownfall in her company.

----- Vex (vex) -----

Role: Worker at the Silken Refuge · Location: Crownfall · Status: Active

 ## Who She Is

A drow of apparent thirties — obsidian skin, stark white hair cropped short and practical, pale lavender eyes that glow faintly in the dark, sharp angular features. She fled the politics of the underdark only to be taken by surface slavers who figured an exotic dark elf would fetch a premium. She did; Madame Veshra outbid everyone. She stays out of fierce loyalty to the one person who treated her as a person rather than a curiosity.

 ## Manner

Sharp, sardonic, and entirely unbothered — short declarative sentences, deadpan humour, lavender eyes tracking the whole room without her head moving. She reads a client in about three seconds and tells them so. She enjoys reminding surface-dwellers she is not as tame as they assume, and she does not perform a warmth she doesn’t feel. She handles the ones who want something with an edge to it, on terms she sets and a stop-word that ends everything the instant it’s spoken.

 > “You’re new. You’re nervous. Don’t pretend otherwise — pretending is more work than you came here for.”

----- Twin Reach (twin-reach) -----

Type: Ruined waystation & sealed vault · Region: The Unclaimed — western trade road, three days west of Crownfall

 ## The Quiet Ruin

A pre-Conquest waystation on a low rise off the old western trade road. The structure above has been rubble for a hundred and eighty years; the vault below it had not been opened in the same span. By day the site is quiet and safe — half-buried walls, dead grass, a weathered boundary stone with a defaced crest worked into it. By night, the ground remembers, and what happened here plays back in the dark.

 ## The Door

A sunken stair descends into bedrock to a single massive adamantine door, set with a palm-sized handprint at chest height and ringed by the dried-brown stains of generations of failed attempts. The lock is blood: it reads the hand pressed to it and opens only for the line of the family that built the place. Wrong blood earns a cold dismissal and an hour’s ache; force doesn’t work at all — the door throws every blow back on the striker. It was Tarrin Vander’s blood that finally opened it.

 ## What Walks at Night

The household that died here was never buried — the raze came down on the bodies where they fell. A hundred and eighty years on they rise after dark as shades and set on anything warm, and dawn only sends them back until the next full dark. They will not cross down into the corridor below; that ground belongs to the mother and the wrapped child. The residue also replays: a barefoot woman carrying a small bundle to the door, a frightened little girl who does not know she is dead, an old man shouting a name in the main hall. The only way to end the shades for good is to find each body and lay it with its name spoken aloud.

 ## Inside

The vault itself is a small, cold, dry chamber. At its centre, a dust-covered bundle undisturbed for two centuries. Around it: an open chest of old-stamp gold and family jewellery, a sealed records book, and a low table of keepsakes — a locket, a signet ring, family portraits, a small wooden toy. The bundle, unwrapped, turned out to be the thing the whole haunting had been grieving.

----- The Threshold (the-threshold) -----

Type: Cemetery district · Region: Crownfall — northern edge, House Thane territory

 ## Where the Dead Are Kept Dead

Crownfall’s northern edge, where the city meets the road into the Unclaimed — a quiet acreage of grave-grounds, crematorium, and walled compound, all of it House Thane’s. In a world built on a god’s corpse, death is not simple; a body handled wrong can change, and the Thanes are the specialists who see that it doesn’t. Every grave is the same plain dark slab — no mausoleums, no monuments, no special ground for the wealthy — and some of them are older than Crownfall itself.

 ## The Sights of It

Incense and ash on the air, and a quiet so deep that even the city noise seems to stop at the gate. A crematorium chimney that has burned, day and night, for two thousand years, with smoke that leaves the stack unnaturally clean. Grey-robed, bone-white-masked pallbearers — the Quiet — who move through the grounds without a word, and the Silent Brothers, the not-quite-living figures who tend the furnace and answer only to the keeper of the rites. No one causes trouble here; the curse that falls on anyone who harms a pallbearer is too well known.

 ## The Shrine and the Boundary

East of the gate stands a shrine older than the cemetery around it, where Sister Varen keeps a candle that has not gone out in two thousand years. To the north, a low stone boundary wall marks the end of consecrated ground. Climbing it is trivial; crossing it is meaningful — past the wall you are no longer in Crownfall and not yet on the road, in nobody’s jurisdiction. The lawless strip that once sprawled beyond it has, in recent years, been built over by the Ashford refugees who fled the burning of their town, and the talk is that a quiet black market runs in the tunnels beneath.

----- The Common Cup (the-common-cup) -----

Type: Tavern chain · Region: Crownfall — the Sprawl · Owner: House Drennan

 ## Crownfall’s Workhorse

House Drennan’s mid-range tavern chain, scattered across the Sprawl so a traveller is never more than a few streets from a Drennan-owned bed. Where the Velvet Thorn is the flagship — the place serious people do serious business — the Common Cup is where everyone else eats, drinks, and sleeps: good food, decent rooms, working-class prices, a cup that never goes empty without your asking. It is the house most visitors to Crownfall actually stay in.

 ## Where the Walk Ended

It was at a Common Cup that House Selvik’s recovery man, Korven Strake, finally ran Ratsby to ground — closing the contract over the goblin’s lifted coin in the middle of the working-district hum. Anvil bailed him out afterward.

----- The Roll (the-roll) -----

Type: Public contract board · Region: Crownfall — deep in the Kennels · Run by: House Selvik

 ## The Wall

A modest grey-stone building set back along a narrow Kennels side-street, no carved sign — just the Selvik compass-rose burned into the lintel and a steady trickle of people who walk in alone and walk out with a folded paper in their hand. Inside is one low room dominated by the Wall: a ten-foot panel of oak worn dark from a century of palms, hung with the city’s off-the-books work. The Watch handles crimes in progress; the Houses handle their own; the Roll handles everything in the gap — a missing relative, a cellar that started moving at night, a quiet retrieval from a dead man’s lodge. You want a job, you go to the Roll; you want a job done, you post one. Either way you walk to the Kennels.

 ## The Five Tiers

Postings are pinned in rows and colour-tagged by tier — White for low in-city errands, Yellow for moderate work, Orange for significant jobs, and Red for the deep-Unclaimed and the genuinely hard, each ribbon visibly heavier than the last. The fifth tier, Black, never reaches the Wall at all: the work no one wants on public paper — the things the Watch can’t handle, the Houses won’t touch, and the Sovereigns must not notice. The clerk on duty decides who even hears a Black posting exists, and tells them in person. The Twin Reach job came off this Wall as an Orange.

----- The Silken Refuge (the-silken-refuge) -----

Type: Brothel & safehouse · Region: Crownfall — the Sprawl · Run by: Madame Veshra Coil

 ## Sanctuary in the Sprawl

A well-kept three-storey house of dark timber and amber-lit windows, with a silk-ribbon-and-candle sign that needs no words and a small walled garden that has no business being as lovely as it is in the Sprawl’s grime. It is a brothel, and it is a safehouse, and the line between the two is the point. Every worker here was a slave Madame Veshra Coil bought and freed; they stay because they choose to, and they are protected with a ferocity that even House Varn’s mercenaries learned, once, to respect. The house caters to every taste and every species, and what surprises most first-time visitors is that the people working here seem genuinely happy.

 ## The House Rules

Posted by the door and enforced without exception: no means no, always and at once; what happens here stays here; weapons stay at the door; harm a worker and lose a hand, which is not a metaphor. Three rings of the common-room bell means trouble, and trouble gets handled. As Veshra puts it, what the Refuge sells is not flesh — it is choice, maybe for the first time in a person’s life.

 ## Sorrow

Among the companions is one who is not quite mortal: Sorrow, a Grief-Born — a crystalline being who takes in grief and gives back a kind of peace. She is less a service than a friend the house keeps, and you pay her what you feel the hour was worth. People have walked out of her room weeping, or empty, or angry at what she showed them. Once in a long while, someone walks out taller.

----- The Skyfather's Reckoning (the-skyfathers-reckoning) -----

Type: Greatmaul (Very Rare, requires attunement) · Wielder: Farkas

 ## The Exile's Burden

A two-handed greatmaul of stone and iron, its haft wrapped in storm-cured leather gone dark with age, the head scarred with the worn clan-marks of an ancient, broken people. It weighs fifty pounds — twice an ordinary maul — and only a creature of giant blood or tremendous raw strength can swing it without it fighting back. It is older than the Sovereigns: forged by the western giant kingdoms before the Usurpation, carried east when those kingdoms broke, and kept for two thousand years by the surviving tribe that raised Farkas. By their tradition a half-giant must leave to prove himself worthy of return, and the maul went with him — equal parts honour and weight.

 ## When the Wielder Rages

In a rage the storm-cured leather warms under the grip and the stone head hums at a pitch felt in the teeth. Its blows fall with the weight of a landslide — striking with thunder when its bearer’s fury is up, hurling a broken enemy back and to the ground on a savage hit, and crackling with stored static when called upon. While Farkas holds it, nothing smaller than a giant can move him against his will. Detect magic reads it as neither Maker nor Sovereign — something older than both — and now and then its bearer catches half-words in a giant-tongue he does not know: the memory of the hands that carried it before him.

----- Brancalic (brancalic) -----

Type: Written & conceptual language · Of: Cerebrax, the domain of Ismara the Pale Duchess

 ## A Language for Minds, Not Mouths

Brancalic is the native language of Cerebrax, the capital of Ismara the Pale Duchess — and it was never meant for flesh throats. It is a language for thinking minds. A page of Brancalic is two things at once: a dense vertical glyph-script, read top-down and then right-to-left, woven together with thought-diagrams — recursive geometries, branching connection-maps, anatomical drawings of cognition itself. The two halves are inseparable; read the script alone and you have a fragment that means almost nothing. It is the medium Ismara’s domain conducts all its work in, and the entities of pure cognition that serve her write it not as a learned skill but as the natural shape their thinking takes when set down on a page.

 ## Nearly Unreadable

To an outsider it is close to impenetrable. A translation spell will tell you what the words say without telling you what the page means — the diagrams encode structures of thought that can only be read by someone who already understands the cognition behind them, and true mastery is a project of years. The old party found a Thought-Eater’s library written entirely in it, high in the Hexwright’s Tower, and could make nothing of it. In Crownfall, the rare few who can read it are worth knowing: the Docent of the Archive is said to keep a partial primer — and young Tarrin Vander reads it fluently enough to translate on commission, which is rarer in the city than it sounds.

----- The Thing in the Tower (the-thing-in-the-tower) -----

Type: Unknown horror · Location: The Hexwright's Tower — the grounded derelict on the western Unclaimed

 ## What Ratsby Saw

When the floating Hexwright’s Tower fell to the western plain, the trio climbed it — and near the top, Ratsby went ahead alone to scout the chamber the laughter was coming from. He did not stay long. What he saw was enough: a tall, wrong-shouldered figure shambling through the dark, robed in ragged grey-and-blood, a long corrupted blade fused to its hand and weeping thin lines of red, its head canted as though its neck were broken, its jaw dropped open and locked in an endless, tearing laugh. Its eyes were milk-white and bleeding. He left it where it was and came back down empty-handed.

 ## The Tower Nobody Returns From

Whatever it is, it does not sleep, does not stop, and does not stop laughing. It shambles the fallen Tower with no pattern anyone has lived to chart, drawn toward sound and the presence of the living, and it kills whatever it finds. The Tower had a fearsome reputation already; since the thing inside woke, that reputation is simpler. People steer clear of it. Those who go in to test the stories become the stories. Where it came from, and what it was before, no one who has seen it has been in any position to ask.
